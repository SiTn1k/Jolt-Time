# 1. Project Vision

## What is Jolt Time

Jolt Time is an educational entertainment Telegram Mini App where players become time travelers on a mission to preserve humanity's greatest achievements. Through exploring historical eras, discovering artifacts, and building personal museums, players experience the wonder of human civilization's greatest moments.

This is not a clicker game. This is not a hamster clone. This is a living museum that travels through time.

## Why This Game Exists

In an age of fragmented attention and shallow entertainment, Jolt Time exists to:

- **Reignite Curiosity** — Every artifact has a story worth telling. Every era holds secrets waiting to be discovered.
- **Make Learning Irresistible** — History becomes an adventure, not a chore. Players absorb knowledge because they *want* to, not because they *have* to.
- **Honor Human Achievement** — From the first cave paintings to the moon landing, from the Library of Alexandria to the Renaissance, Jolt Time celebrates the moments that defined us.
- **Create Lasting Value** — Unlike temporary entertainment, a completed collection remains meaningful. Unlike consumable content, every artifact becomes a permanent treasure.

## Why Players Should Return Every Day

**The Hook:**

- Daily expeditions reveal new artifacts waiting to be discovered
- Streak rewards compound the longer players commit
- New historical eras unlock as collections grow
- Limited-time events introduce exclusive artifacts that cannot be obtained elsewhere
- Friend leaderboards create gentle competitive pressure
- Seasonal content resets progression with fresh goals

**The Promise:**

Every login advances the museum. Every session adds to the story. Every return brings new discoveries.

Players return because their museum is alive, growing, and reflecting their journey through time.

# 2. Core Fantasy

## The Player Fantasy

When a player opens Jolt Time, they are not playing a game. They are stepping into a role.

## Explorer

The player is an explorer venturing into uncharted temporal territories. Each expedition takes them deeper into history, revealing civilizations lost to time and artifacts buried by millennia. The thrill of discovery drives every expedition.

## Historian

The player becomes a historian cataloging the greatest moments of human achievement. Every artifact tells a story. Every collection piece together the grand narrative of who we are and where we came from.

## Collector

The player is a curator assembling the world's most comprehensive personal collection. From common relics to mythic treasures, every acquisition fills a gap in the museum's grand design.

## Museum Curator

The player designs and builds their own museum, arranging artifacts by era, civilization, or personal theme. The museum becomes an expression of the player's identity and taste.

## Time Traveler

The player manipulates time itself, sending expeditions to different eras and witnessing history unfold. Time is not a river but a vast ocean to be navigated.

## Civilisation Builder

The player contributes to restoring the timeline by recovering lost artifacts and preserving historical knowledge. Each restoration strengthens the fabric of history against temporal anomalies.

# 4. Game Identity

## Why Jolt Time is Different

### Not Hamster

Hamster Kombat is a single-session clicker with no collection, no progression depth, and no educational value. It is pure dopamine extraction.

**Jolt Time** is a collection-driven experience where every session builds toward permanent progress. The museum is the goal, and it never resets.

### Not TapSwap

TapSwap is a repetitive motion game built on hype and referral mechanics. There is no depth, no story, no meaning.

**Jolt Time** tells the greatest story ever told — the story of humanity — through artifacts that have meaning beyond pixels.

### Not Notcoin

Notcoin gamified nothing beyond tapping. It was a channel for speculative distribution with no craft, no curation, no soul.

**Jolt Time** curates real history. Every artifact is a real object with real significance. The collection reflects actual human achievement.

### Not Simple Clickers

Clicker games reward reflexive motion. Progression is linear. Repetition is the mechanic.

**Jolt Time** rewards exploration, strategy, and patience. Different artifacts require different approaches. Time investment is meaningful.

### Not Idle Games

Idle games automate progress in exchange for absence. Players watch numbers increase without engagement.

**Jolt Time** rewards engagement. Every expedition is a choice. Every collection decision matters. The player is always the driver.

### Not Museum Simulators

Museum simulators are passive displays. They show artifacts without context, history without adventure.

**Jolt Time** makes the museum *alive*. Artifacts are discovered, not purchased. Stories are uncovered, not read. The player is the museum's creator and narrator.

## The Jolt Time Difference

| Aspect | Other Games | Jolt Time |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Progression** | Resets or stagnates | Permanent, meaningful |
| **Collection** | Cosmetic or meaningless | Educational, permanent |
| **Learning** | None | Embedded, discoverable |
| **Engagement** | Repetitive motion | Strategic choice |
| **Community** | Referral chains | Shared discovery |
| **Value** | Temporary hype | Lasting collection |

# 5. Player Emotions

## Curiosity

*"What will I find in Ancient Egypt? What secrets does the Renaissance hold?"*

Curiosity drives every expedition. The unknown is always beckoning. Every era promises discoveries that have never been seen before.

## Discovery

*"I found it! The Sphinx amulet!"*

Discovery is the heart of Jolt Time. Finding an artifact — especially a rare one — triggers genuine excitement. The moment of revelation is sacred.

## Achievement

*"I completed the Mesopotamian collection. All 12 artifacts secured."*

Achievement is measured in completions, percentages, and milestones. Every goal reached is a testament to the player's dedication.

## Progression

*"My museum just hit 50% complete. The halls are filling up."*

Progression is visible, tangible, and permanent. The museum grows. The collection deepens. The player advances.

## Collection

*"I am 3 artifacts away from completing the Classical Era. So close."*

Collection creates attachment. Each artifact is a piece of the puzzle. Completism becomes a personal mission.

## Exploration

*"What happens if I send an expedition to the Industrial Age? What will I find?"*

Exploration is both physical (traveling to eras) and intellectual (learning about artifacts). The player is always discovering something new.

## Pride

*"This is my museum. I built this."*

The museum is a personal creation. It reflects the player's journey, choices, and dedication. Pride in one's collection is the ultimate reward.

## Competition

*"I am in the top 100 collectors worldwide. Can I reach the top 50?"*

Leaderboards create gentle competitive pressure. Guilds foster team achievement. Tournaments satisfy the competitive spirit.

## Long-Term Growth

*"I started 6 months ago with nothing. Now my museum has artifacts from 8 civilizations. Where will I be in a year?"*

Jolt Time is a long-term commitment. Players see themselves growing alongside their collection. The journey is as rewarding as the destination.

# 6. Target Audience

## Primary Audience

### Telegram Users

Over 700 million active users on Telegram. Players who prefer the convenience of mini apps within their existing communication platform. They want entertainment without friction.

### Casual Players

Players who want to enjoy a game without intense commitment. Sessions are short. Progress is meaningful. No FOMO when away for a day.

### Idle Players

Players who enjoy watching progress accumulate. They check in regularly but do not need to grind. They appreciate systems that reward consistent attention.

### History Lovers

Players who are genuinely interested in history, archaeology, and human civilization. They play to learn while they play. Educational value is a feature, not a bug.

### Collectors

Players who derive satisfaction from completing sets, finding rare items, and organizing collections. The museum is their gallery. Artifacts are their treasures.

## Secondary Audience

### Strategy Players

Players who enjoy optimizing their approach, planning expeditions, and managing resources. They appreciate depth beneath the accessible surface.

### Completionists

Players who must complete everything. Every artifact must be collected. Every era must be mastered. 100% completion is the only acceptable goal.

### PvP Players

Players who thrive on competition. They want leaderboards that matter, tournaments that challenge, and rewards that recognize skill and dedication.

# 7. Player Motivation

## Why Players Return

### Daily Return Motivations

- **Daily Streak** — Consecutive login rewards that compound over time
- **Energy Cap** — Energy regenerates and caps; players return to spend it
- **Daily Expedition** — One guaranteed artifact from daily expeditions
- **Daily Challenge** — Small task with small reward, creating habit
- **Friend Interaction** — Send/receive gifts, compete on leaderboards

### Weekly Return Motivations

- **Weekly Quests** — Longer objectives requiring multiple sessions
- **Weekly Tournaments** — Competitive events with leaderboard resets
- **New Content** — Weekly artifact additions or era updates
- **Guild Activities** — Group objectives and collective rewards
- **Battle Pass Progress** — Seasonal track requiring weekly engagement

### Monthly Return Motivations

- **Season End** — Major rewards for Battle Pass and seasonal artifacts
- **Era Unlocks** — New historical eras becoming available
- **Collection Milestones** — Major completion thresholds reached
- **Anniversary Events** — Special celebrations with exclusive rewards
- **Major Updates** — Feature drops and content expansions

### Yearly Return Motivations

- **Major Expansions** — Substantial new content (new civilizations, new features)
- **Global Events** — Worldwide competitions with exclusive rewards
- **Anniversary Celebrations** — Yearly milestones with commemorative artifacts
- **Legacy Recognition** — Long-term player rewards and recognition
- **Collection Completions** — Rare players achieving 100% collection

# 8. Design Philosophy

## The Golden Rules

### Progress Always Matters

Every action advances the player toward a meaningful goal. Nothing is wasted. Time invested is time well spent.

### Nothing Feels Wasted

Duplicates become fragments. Failed expeditions yield resources. Every outcome contributes to the collection. No frustration, only progress.

### Collection is Meaningful

Every artifact tells a story. Every completion feels earned. The collection is not just a number — it is a narrative of the player's journey.

### Every Artifact Tells a Story

The Terracotta Army soldier is not just "Rare Artifact #47." It is a guardian of an emperor's tomb, a testament to the ambition of Qin Shi Huang. Context transforms acquisition into discovery.

### Learning Must Be Fun

Education is not a separate mode. It is woven into the experience. Players learn because the game rewards curiosity, not because it demands memorization.

### History Must Feel Alive

History is not a textbook. It is a living, breathing adventure. Eras are not locked screens — they are worlds to explore. Artifacts are not items — they are windows into past lives.

### Time is a Resource

Time in Jolt Time has weight. Expeditions take time. Energy regenerates with time. Progression respects the player's time investment.

### Discovery is Sacred

The moment of finding a new artifact must feel special. Animations, sounds, and feedback elevate acquisition into celebration.

### The Museum is Personal

Every player's museum is unique. Display choices, favorite artifacts, and collection focus express individual identity.

### Permanence Creates Value

What is earned stays earned. What is collected remains collected. Jolt Time does not reset progress. Completions are forever.

# 9. World Philosophy

## The Museum is Alive

The museum is not a static display. It breathes. It grows. It responds.

- Artifacts glow when newly acquired
- Completed eras receive special animations
- Rare discoveries trigger celebration effects
- The museum evolves alongside the player

## Time is Alive

Time in Jolt Time is not a static backdrop. It flows.

- Day/night cycles affect expedition visuals
- Seasonal themes transform the interface
- Historical events align with real-world commemorations
- Time travel feels consequential

## History Changes

History is not fixed. It is discovered.

- New archaeological discoveries update artifact descriptions
- Era introductions evolve as knowledge expands
- Educational content improves over time
- Community discoveries contribute to collective knowledge

## Every Civilisation Matters

From Sumer to China, from Egypt to Rome, from the Mayans to the Vikings — every civilization contributes to the human story. No culture is marginalized. No achievement is overlooked.

## Everything Connects

Artifacts are not isolated. They are nodes in a web of meaning.

- Related artifacts cross-reference each other
- Civilizations influence each other across eras
- Artifacts from the same period tell different stories
- The collection becomes a living timeline

# 13. Monetization Philosophy

## Core Principles

### Fair

Players who pay do not gain unfair advantages.

- No power purchased with money
- No exclusive gameplay-affecting items
- No paywalls blocking content
- Time is the only true currency

### Cosmetic

Monetization enhances but never defines.

- Profile decorations and frames
- Museum display customizations
- Expedition visual upgrades
- Collection showcase options

### Convenience

Paying saves time, not skill.

- Faster energy regeneration
- Additional expedition slots
- Priority queue for quests
- Convenience features only

### Never Pay To Win

The leaderboard always reflects dedication.

- Competition is skill and time-based
- Spending provides no competitive advantage
- Free players can achieve everything
- Leaderboard integrity is sacred

### Respect Player Time

Monetization never punishes free play.

- Free players progress at meaningful pace
- Optional ads never interrupt
- No artificial waiting walls
- Every hour played matters
