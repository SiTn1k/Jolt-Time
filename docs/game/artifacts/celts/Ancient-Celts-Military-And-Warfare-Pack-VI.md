# Ancient Celts - Military & Warfare Pack VI

## Artifacts

### 1. **La Tène Iron Sword**
- **Original Celtic Name**: *Kladiwos* (reconstructed)
- **Civilization**: Ancient Celts
- **Historical Period**: La Tène Culture
- **Approximate Date**: 3rd century BCE
- **Current Museum**: Laténium, Switzerland
- **Discovery Site**: La Tène archaeological site, Switzerland
- **Category**: Iron Swords
- **Material**: Iron, Bronze (hilt decorations)
- **Dimensions**: Length: 75 cm
- **Historical Description**: A leaf-shaped iron sword with a decorated bronze hilt, typical of Celtic warriors.
- **Military Context**: Used by elite warriors in close combat, symbolizing status and skill.
- **Historical Importance**: Exemplifies Celtic metallurgical mastery and warrior culture.
- **Discovery Story**: Found in a river deposit, likely a ritual offering.
- **Interesting Facts**: The blade shows signs of sharpening and combat use.
- **Educational Summary**: Highlights the role of swords in Celtic warfare and society.
- **References**: La Tène excavation reports, Laténium museum catalog.

### 2. **Battersea Shield**
- **Original Celtic Name**: Unknown
- **Civilization**: Ancient Celts
- **Historical Period**: Late La Tène Culture
- **Approximate Date**: 1st century BCE
- **Current Museum**: British Museum, UK
- **Discovery Site**: River Thames, Battersea, UK
- **Category**: Shields
- **Material**: Bronze, Wood (core, now decayed)
- **Dimensions**: Height: 77 cm, Width: 35 cm
- **Historical Description**: A ceremonial bronze shield with intricate repoussé designs.
- **Military Context**: Likely used for display or ritual, reflecting Celtic artistry and symbolism.
- **Historical Importance**: One of the finest surviving Celtic shields.
- **Discovery Story**: Recovered from the Thames during dredging in 1857.
- **Interesting Facts**: The shield's design includes spirals and animal motifs.
- **Educational Summary**: Demonstrates the blend of function and artistry in Celtic military gear.
- **References**: British Museum catalog, peer-reviewed studies on Celtic shields.

### 3. **Carnyx War Horn from Deskford**
- **Original Celtic Name**: *Karnyx* (reconstructed)
- **Civilization**: Ancient Celts
- **Historical Period**: Late La Tène Culture
- **Approximate Date**: 1st century CE
- **Current Museum**: National Museum of Scotland
- **Discovery Site**: Deskford, Scotland
- **Category**: Carnyx War Horns
- **Material**: Bronze, Wood (mouthpiece)
- **Dimensions**: Length: 1.8 m
- **Historical Description**: A boar-headed war horn used for signaling in battle.
- **Military Context**: Played to intimidate enemies and coordinate troops.
- **Historical Importance**: Rare surviving example of a Celtic musical instrument of war.
- **Discovery Story**: Found in a peat bog, preserved by anaerobic conditions.
- **Interesting Facts**: The boar's head is detachable for transport.
- **Educational Summary**: Illustrates the psychological and tactical aspects of Celtic warfare.
- **References**: Deskford excavation reports, National Museum of Scotland catalog.
