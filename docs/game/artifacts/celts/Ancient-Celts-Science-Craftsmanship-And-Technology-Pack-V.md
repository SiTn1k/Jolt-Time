# Ancient Celts - Science, Craftsmanship & Technology Pack V

## Artifacts

### 1. **Iron Smelting Furnace from Kelheim**
- **Original Celtic Name**: Unknown
- **Civilization**: Ancient Celts
- **Historical Period**: La Tène Culture
- **Approximate Date**: 3rd century BCE
- **Current Museum**: Archaeological Museum of Kelheim, Germany
- **Discovery Site**: Oppidum of Kelheim, Germany
- **Category**: Iron Smelting Furnaces
- **Material**: Clay, Iron slag
- **Dimensions**: Diameter: 50 cm, Height: 60 cm
- **Historical Description**: A well-preserved iron smelting furnace used for producing iron blooms.
- **Technological Context**: Demonstrates advanced metallurgical techniques, including temperature control and slag management.
- **Historical Importance**: Key evidence of Celtic iron production capabilities.
- **Discovery Story**: Excavated from an industrial zone within the oppidum.
- **Interesting Facts**: The furnace design influenced later Roman ironworking.
- **Educational Summary**: Highlights Celtic contributions to early metallurgy.
- **References**: Kelheim excavation reports, Archaeological Museum of Kelheim catalog.
