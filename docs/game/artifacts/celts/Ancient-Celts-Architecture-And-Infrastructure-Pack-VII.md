# Ancient Celts - Architecture, Engineering & Infrastructure Pack VII

## Artifacts

### 1. **Heuneburg Gate & Walls**
- **Original Celtic Name**: *Dūnon* (reconstructed term for fortified settlement)
- **Civilization**: Ancient Celts
- **Historical Period**: Early La Tène Culture
- **Approximate Date**: 6th century BCE
- **Current Museum**: Heuneburg Museum, Germany
- **Discovery Site**: Heuneburg hillfort, Germany
- **Category**: Hillfort Gates & Walls
- **Material**: Limestone, Timber, Clay
- **Dimensions**: Wall height: 4 m (reconstructed)
- **Historical Description**: A monumental mudbrick wall with timber framing, inspired by Mediterranean architecture.
- **Engineering Context**: Demonstrates Celtic adaptation of foreign techniques for large-scale fortifications.
- **Historical Importance**: Among the earliest Celtic proto-urban structures.
- **Discovery Story**: Excavated in the 20th century with preserved stratigraphy.
- **Interesting Facts**: Featured a Greek-style projecting bastion.
- **Educational Summary**: Illustrates early Celtic urbanization and defensive innovation.
- **References**: Heuneburg excavation publications, museum catalogs.
