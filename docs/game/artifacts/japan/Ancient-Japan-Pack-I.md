### Artifacts Preview

1. Name: Bronze Mirror from Sakitama Kofun
   Original Japanese Name: 埼玉古墳群出土の銅鏡
   Civilization: Ancient Japan
   Historical Period: Kofun
   Approximate Date: 5th century CE
   Current Museum: Saitama Prefectural Museum
   Discovery Site: Sakitama Kofun Cluster
   Category: Burial Offering
   Material: Bronze
   Dimensions: Diameter 18 cm
   Historical Description: A finely cast bronze mirror decorated with mythological motifs, buried as a status symbol.
   Historical Context: Mirrors symbolized imperial authority and were key burial goods for elite tombs.
   Historical Importance: Demonstrates Chinese cultural influence and early Japanese state formation.
   Discovery Story: Excavated in 1968 from Sakitama Kofun Cluster.
   Interesting Facts: Similar mirrors found in Korean peninsula show cultural connections.
   Educational Summary: Highlights political symbolism and burial customs of Kofun period elites.
   References:
   - Saitama Prefectural Museum catalog
   - Barnes, Gina (2015). "Archaeology of East Asia"
   - Peer-reviewed archaeological reports
