## Artifacts

### 1. Takshashila Surgical Instruments
- **Original Sanskrit Name**: शल्य यन्त्र (Shalya Yantra)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Maurya Empire
- **Approximate Date**: 300 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Taxila Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Taxila
- **Category**: Bronze Surgical Tools
- **Material**: Bronze
- **Dimensions**: Varies (5-15 cm)
- **Historical Description**: Set of surgical instruments including forceps, scalpels, and probes.
- **Scientific/Medical Context**: Used for complex surgeries described in Sushruta Samhita.
- **Historical Importance**: Earliest known surgical tools in South Asia.
- **Discovery Story**: Found in medical college ruins at Taxila.
- **Interesting Facts**: Includes tools for cataract surgery and rhinoplasty.
- **Educational Summary**: Demonstrates advanced ancient surgical knowledge.
- **References**:
  - Taxila Museum Catalogue
  - "Ancient Indian Medicine" by A.L. Basham
