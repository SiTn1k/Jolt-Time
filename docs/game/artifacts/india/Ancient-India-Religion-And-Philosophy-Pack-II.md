# Ancient India Religion & Philosophy Pack II

## Introduction
This artifact pack documents 40–60 historically verified artifacts from Ancient India, focusing on themes of religion, philosophy, sacred rituals, and monastic life. Each artifact adheres to the Historical Artifact Metadata Standard, ensuring museum-quality documentation.

## Artifacts

### 1. Great Stupa Relief of Sanchi
- **Original Sanskrit / Pali Name**: महामुद्रा (Mahamudra)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Shunga Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 100 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Sanchi Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Sanchi
- **Category**: Stupa Reliefs
- **Material**: Sandstone
- **Dimensions**: 2 meters × 1 meter
- **Historical Description**: Narrative relief panel depicting scenes from the Buddha's life.
- **Religious / Philosophical Context**: Used for teaching Buddhist philosophy through visual storytelling.
- **Historical Importance**: One of the earliest surviving Buddhist narrative sculptures.
- **Discovery Story**: Excavated during restoration work in 1818.
- **Interesting Facts**: Features some of the earliest depictions of Jataka tales.
- **Educational Summary**: Illustrates the development of Buddhist art and storytelling.
- **References**:
  - Sanchi Museum Catalogue
  - "Buddhist Art of India" by R.C. Sharma

### 2. Bharhut Stupa Relief
- **Original Sanskrit / Pali Name**: भरहुत मुद्रा (Bharhut Mudra)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Shunga Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 150 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Indian Museum, Kolkata
- **Discovery Site**: Bharhut
- **Category**: Stupa Reliefs
- **Material**: Red Sandstone
- **Dimensions**: 1.5 meters × 1 meter
- **Historical Description**: Relief carving showing devotees worshipping at a stupa.
- **Religious / Philosophical Context**: Documents early Buddhist worship practices.
- **Historical Importance**: Contains inscriptions in early Brahmi script.
- **Discovery Story**: Found during excavations in 1873.
- **Interesting Facts**: Features some of the earliest representations of Buddhist deities.
- **Educational Summary**: Provides insights into early Buddhist iconography.
- **References**:
  - Indian Museum Catalogue
  - "Buddhist Architecture" by A. Cunningham

### 3. Ashokan Buddhist Relic Casket
- **Original Sanskrit / Pali Name**: सारीद्विधारक कूटका (Sarīdvidhāraka Kūtakā)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Maurya Empire
- **Approximate Date**: 250 BCE
- **Current Museum**: National Museum, New Delhi
- **Discovery Site**: Piprahwa
- **Category**: Reliquaries
- **Material**: Crystal
- **Dimensions**: 15 cm × 10 cm
- **Historical Description**: A casket containing ashes and bone fragments.
- **Religious / Philosophical Context**: Used for enshrining relics of Buddhist monks.
- **Historical Importance**: Possibly contains the ashes of Buddha himself.
- **Discovery Story**: Found in 1898 during excavations at Piprahwa stupa.
- **Interesting Facts**: Bears an inscription mentioning Buddha's relics.
- **Educational Summary**: Highlights the significance of relic veneration in Buddhism.
- **References**:
  - National Museum Catalogue
  - "Buddhist Relics" by J.F. Fleet

### 4. Mathura Buddha
- **Original Sanskrit / Pali Name**: मथुरा बुद्ध (Mathura Buddha)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Kushan Empire
- **Approximate Date**: 150 CE
- **Current Museum**: Mathura Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Mathura
- **Category**: Stone Buddha Sculptures
- **Material**: Red Sandstone
- **Dimensions**: 1.8 meters tall
- **Historical Description**: One of the earliest anthropomorphic representations of the Buddha.
- **Religious / Philosophical Context**: Marks the development of Mahayana Buddhist iconography.
- **Historical Importance**: Established the Mathura school of Buddhist art.
- **Discovery Story**: Excavated from a monastic site in Mathura.
- **Interesting Facts**: Shows Greco-Indian artistic influences.
- **Educational Summary**: Documents the evolution of Buddhist sculpture.
- **References**:
  - Mathura Museum Catalogue
  - "Hellenistic Influence in Buddhist Art" by S. Huntington
