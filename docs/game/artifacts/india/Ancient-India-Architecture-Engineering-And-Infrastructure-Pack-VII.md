## Introduction
This artifact pack documents 40–60 historically verified artifacts from Ancient India, focusing on architectural achievements, engineering innovations, and urban infrastructure. Each artifact adheres to the Historical Artifact Metadata Standard, ensuring museum-quality documentation.

## Artifacts

### 1. Great Bath Bricks (Mohenjo-daro)
- **Original Name**: महानाद इट्टी (Mahānād Iṭṭī)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Indus Valley Civilization
- **Approximate Date**: 2500 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Mohenjo-daro Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Mohenjo-daro
- **Category**: Baked Bricks
- **Material**: Fired Brick
- **Dimensions**: 28 cm × 14 cm × 7 cm
- **Historical Description**: Standardized bricks used in the Great Bath construction.
- **Architectural Context**: Waterproof construction with bitumen lining.
- **Historical Importance**: Earliest public water tank in South Asia.
- **Discovery Story**: Excavated by John Marshall in 1920s.
- **Interesting Facts**: Precise 4:2:1 ratio standardization.
- **Educational Summary**: Demonstrates advanced hydraulic engineering.
- **References**:
  - Mohenjo-daro Site Reports
  - "Indus Valley Civilization" by J.M. Kenoyer

### 2. Indus Drainage Pipe
- **Original Name**: नाली (Nālī)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Indus Valley Civilization
- **Approximate Date**: 2300 BCE
- **Current Museum**: National Museum, New Delhi
- **Discovery Site**: Harappa
- **Category**: Drainage Pipes
- **Material**: Terracotta
- **Dimensions**: 30 cm long, 15 cm diameter
- **Historical Description**: Cylindrical pipe with flanged joints.
- **Architectural Context**: Part of sophisticated sewer system.
- **Historical Importance**: Early evidence of urban sanitation.
- **Discovery Story**: Found in street excavations.
- **Interesting Facts**: Designed with inspection holes.
- **Educational Summary**: Shows advanced urban planning.
- **References**:
  - National Museum Catalogue
  - "Ancient Indian Technology" by D.K. Chakrabarti

### 3. Ashokan Pillar Capital
- **Original Sanskrit Name**: स्तम्भ शिर्ष (Stambha Śīrṣa)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Maurya Empire
- **Approximate Date**: 250 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Sarnath Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Sarnath
- **Category**: Pillar Capitals
- **Material**: Polished Sandstone
- **Dimensions**: 2.1 meters tall

- **Architectural Context**: Monumental pillar construction.
- **Historical Importance**: National emblem of India.
- **Discovery Story**: Found during 1904 excavations.
- **Interesting Facts**: Transported from Chunar quarries.
- **Educational Summary**: Demonstrates Mauryan engineering.
- **References**:

### 4. Sanchi Stupa Gateway
- **Original Sanskrit Name**: तोरण (Toraṇa)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Shunga Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 100 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Site Museum, Sanchi
- **Discovery Site**: Sanchi
- **Category**: Stupa Gateways
- **Material**: Sandstone
- **Dimensions**: 8 meters tall
- **Historical Description**: Ornate gateway with narrative reliefs.
- **Architectural Context**: Ceremonial entrance to stupa complex.
- **Historical Importance**: Best preserved early Buddhist gateway.
- **Discovery Story**: Restored in 19th century.
- **Interesting Facts**: Contains 500+ individual carvings.
- **Educational Summary**: Shows development of Buddhist architecture.
- **References**:
  - Sanchi Site Reports
  - "Buddhist Architecture" by A. Cunningham
