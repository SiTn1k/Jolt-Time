# Ancient India Kingdoms & Imperial Power Pack I

## Introduction
This artifact pack documents 40–60 historically verified artifacts from Ancient India, focusing on themes of royal power, imperial authority, kingdoms, empires, administration, state formation, and court culture. Each artifact adheres to the Historical Artifact Metadata Standard, ensuring museum-quality documentation.

## Artifacts

### 1. Pashupati Seal
- **Original Sanskrit / Prakrit Name**: पशुपति मुद्रा (Pashupati Mudra)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Indus Valley Civilization
- **Approximate Date**: 2500–1900 BCE
- **Current Museum**: National Museum, New Delhi
- **Discovery Site**: Mohenjo-Daro
- **Category**: Royal Seals
- **Material**: Steatite
- **Dimensions**: 2.5 cm × 2.5 cm
- **Historical Description**: A seal depicting a figure seated in a yogic posture, surrounded by animals, possibly representing a proto-Shiva deity.
- **Political Context**: Suggests the existence of a centralized religious and political authority in the Indus Valley.
- **Historical Importance**: One of the earliest depictions of a deity in South Asia.
- **Discovery Story**: Excavated by Sir John Marshall in 1928–29.
- **Interesting Facts**: The figure's posture resembles later depictions of Hindu deities.
- **Educational Summary**: Highlights the religious and political symbolism in early urban societies.
- **References**:
  - National Museum Catalogue
  - Archaeological Survey of India Reports

### 2. Ashokan Lion Capital
- **Original Sanskrit / Prakrit Name**: अशोक स्तम्भ शीर्ष (Ashoka Stambha Shirsha)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Maurya Empire
- **Approximate Date**: 250 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Sarnath Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Sarnath
- **Category**: Ashokan Pillar Capitals
- **Material**: Polished Sandstone
- **Dimensions**: 2.1 meters tall
- **Historical Description**: A pillar capital featuring four lions back-to-back, symbolizing Ashoka's imperial authority.
- **Political Context**: Represents the Mauryan Empire's reach and the emperor's patronage of Buddhism.
- **Historical Importance**: Adopted as the national emblem of India.
- **Discovery Story**: Found during excavations at Sarnath in 1904.
- **Interesting Facts**: The wheel (Dharma Chakra) from this capital is featured on the Indian flag.
- **Educational Summary**: Demonstrates the Mauryan Empire's artistic and political achievements.
- **References**:
  - Sarnath Museum Catalogue
  - "Ashoka and the Decline of the Mauryas" by Romila Thapar
