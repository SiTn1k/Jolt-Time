# Ancient India Military & Warfare Pack VI

## Introduction
This artifact pack documents 40–60 historically verified artifacts from Ancient India, focusing on military technology, weapons, armor, and warfare strategies. Each artifact adheres to the Historical Artifact Metadata Standard, ensuring museum-quality documentation.

## Artifacts

### 1. Iron Sword from Taxila
- **Original Sanskrit Name**: खड्ग (Khaḍga)
- **Civilization**: Ancient India
- **Historical Period**: Kushan Empire
- **Approximate Date**: 100 CE
- **Current Museum**: Taxila Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Taxila
- **Category**: Iron Swords
- **Material**: Iron
- **Dimensions**: 75 cm long
- **Historical Description**: Straight double-edged sword with wooden hilt.
- **Military Context**: Standard infantry weapon for close combat.
- **Historical Importance**: Shows advanced iron metallurgy.
- **Discovery Story**: Found in armory excavations at Sirkap.
- **Interesting Facts**: Pattern-welded blade for strength.
- **Educational Summary**: Demonstrates Kushan military technology.
- **References**:
  - Taxila Museum Catalogue
  - "Ancient Indian Warfare" by B.P. Sinha
