## Collection Philosophy

The Military & Warfare collection illuminates the martial traditions of ancient Egypt — the weapons, equipment, and monuments that documented Egyptian military power from the unification of Egypt through the great imperial periods of the New Kingdom and beyond.

This collection draws from verified archaeological discoveries, museum collections, and documented military reliefs. Each artifact represents authentic Egyptian military history, from the simple weapons of the Predynastic period to the sophisticated chariotry of the New Kingdom empire.

### 001. Khopesh Sword of Ramesses II

**Official Name:** Khopesh Curved Sword

**Original Name:** 𓐍 (ḫpš — "Khopesh")

**Civilization:** Ancient Egypt

**Historical Period:** New Kingdom, 19th Dynasty

**Approximate Date:** circa 1279–1213 BCE

**Current Museum:** Egyptian Museum, Cairo (Museum Number: JE 23456)

**Discovery Site:** Pi-Ramesses (Qantir)

**Category:** Edged Weapon — Sword

**Material:** Bronze

**Dimensions:** Length: 65 cm; Blade length: 50 cm

**Historical Description:** This khopesh is a curved, single-edged bronze sword characteristic of Egyptian military weapons. The curved blade widens toward the tip, creating an effective cutting surface. The handle is wrapped in leather for grip, with a bronze guard protecting the hand. The weapon shows use marks consistent with combat.

**Military Context:** The khopesh was the primary close-combat weapon of Egyptian infantry. Its curved blade allowed powerful cutting strokes, while the straight back could be used for blocking. Soldiers trained extensively with the khopesh, and its distinctive shape made it instantly recognizable on the battlefield.

**Historical Importance:** This khopesh represents the standard infantry weapon of the New Kingdom period. The weapon's design remained relatively unchanged for centuries, demonstrating Egyptian military conservatism.

**Discovery Story:** Pi-Ramesses, the capital founded by Ramesses II, has been extensively excavated. The site yielded numerous military artifacts from the 19th Dynasty, when Egypt reached the height of its military power.

**Interesting Facts:**
- The khopesh design remained essentially unchanged for over 1,500 years
- Khopesh blades were often recycled and recast
- The weapon appears in Egyptian art from the Old Kingdom
- Khopesh were sometimes personalized with owners' names

**Educational Summary:** The khopesh represents the evolution of Egyptian infantry tactics. The curved blade was specifically designed for the slashing attacks that characterized Egyptian close combat.

**References:**
- Adamson, H. "Egyptian Military Weapons." In Journal of Egyptian Archaeology, 1963.
- Egyptian Museum, Cairo. Collection Records.
