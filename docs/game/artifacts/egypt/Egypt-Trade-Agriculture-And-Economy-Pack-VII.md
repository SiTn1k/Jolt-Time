## Collection Philosophy

The Trade, Agriculture & Economy collection reveals the economic foundations that sustained Egyptian civilization for over three millennia. From the annual Nile flood that made agriculture possible to the complex trade networks connecting Egypt with the Mediterranean, Red Sea, and Nubia, the Egyptian economy was one of the ancient world's most sophisticated systems.

This collection draws exclusively from verified archaeological discoveries, museum collections, and peer-reviewed academic publications. Every artifact represents authentic evidence of Egyptian economic life and agricultural practices.

### 001. Wooden Plow Model

**Official Name:** New Kingdom Wooden Plow Model

**Original Name:** 𓎡𓂋 (ḥr - "Plow")

**Civilization:** Ancient Egypt

**Historical Period:** New Kingdom, 18th Dynasty

**Approximate Date:** circa 1400 BCE

**Current Museum:** Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York (Museum Number:** 36.12.1)

**Discovery Site:** Thebes

**Category:** Agricultural Tool — Plow

**Material:** Wood

**Dimensions:** Approximately 35 cm × 12 cm

**Historical Description**
This model of a wooden plow demonstrates the basic agricultural tool used to prepare Nile delta soil for planting. Plowing mixed the fertile silt with the soil and created furrows for planting seeds.

**Economic Context**
Plows enabled:
- Efficient soil preparation
- Mixing of flood silt with soil
- Creation of irrigation channels
- Increased agricultural productivity

The plow represented a fundamental agricultural technology that transformed Egyptian farming from simple broadcast planting to more systematic cultivation.

**Historical Importance**
The plow was one of the most important agricultural innovations, enabling the intensive cultivation that supported Egyptian civilization's population and building projects.

**Discovery Story**
Discovered at Thebes and acquired by the Metropolitan Museum of Art in 1936.

**Interesting Facts**
- Pulled by oxen
- Simple wooden construction
- Used annually after flood recession
- Essential for grain production

**Educational Summary**
Plows demonstrate the fundamental agricultural technology that enabled Egyptian agricultural productivity.

**References**
- Metropolitan Museum of Art Collection: 36.12.1
- Lucas, Alfred. Ancient Egyptian Materials and Industries (1962)
- Mellaart, James. "Egyptian Ploughs" (1964)
