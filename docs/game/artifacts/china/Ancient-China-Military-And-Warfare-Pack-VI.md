# Ancient China Military & Warfare Pack VI

## Artifact Catalog

### 1. Qin Crossbow Trigger Mechanism
- **Original Chinese Name**: 弩机 (Nu Jī)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Qin Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 221–206 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Terracotta Army Museum, Xi'an
- **Discovery Site**: Pit 1, Terracotta Army Complex
- **Category**: Ranged Weapon
- **Material**: Bronze
- **Dimensions**: 15.4 × 3.2 cm
- **Historical Description**: Precision-made trigger assembly for Qin repeating crossbows
- **Military Context**: Gave Qin armies tactical advantage with rapid firing capability
- **Historical Importance**: Key to Qin unification victories
- **Discovery Story**: Found with terracotta archers in 1974
- **Interesting Facts**: Contains 3 moving parts with precise tolerances
- **Educational Summary**: Demonstrates Qin military technological superiority
- **References**:
  - Terracotta Army excavation reports
  - "Qin Military Technology" by Li Yufang

### 2. Shang Bronze Ge Halberd
- **Original Chinese Name**: 戊 (Gē)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Shang Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 1200 BCE
- **Current Museum**: National Museum of China
- **Discovery Site**: Yinxu, Anyang
- **Category**: Polearm
- **Material**: Bronze
- **Dimensions**: 27.5 cm blade
- **Historical Description**: Ceremonial/combat halberd with taotie motifs
- **Military Context**: Elite weapon showing early Chinese metallurgical mastery
- **Historical Importance**: Earliest Chinese polearm design
- **Discovery Story**: Found in royal tomb with other ritual weapons
- **Interesting Facts**: Inscriptions name royal guards
- **Educational Summary**: Shows Shang military-ceremonial complex
- **References**:
  - Anyang excavation reports
  - "Shang Weapons" by Yang Boda
