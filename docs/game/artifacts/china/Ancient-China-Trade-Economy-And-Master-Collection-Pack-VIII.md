## PART A: Trade & Economy Artifact Pack

### 1. Han Dynasty Wu Zhu Coin
- **Original Chinese Name**: 五铢钱 (Wǔ Zhū Qián)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Han Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 118 BCE–220 CE
- **Current Museum**: National Museum of China
- **Discovery Site**: Chang'an mint site
- **Category**: Currency
- **Material**: Bronze
- **Dimensions**: 2.5 cm diameter
- **Historical Description**: Standardized coin with weight-based value
- **Economic Context**: Foundation of Han monetary system for 400 years
- **Historical Importance**: First stable imperial currency
- **Discovery Story**: Found in merchant graves along Silk Road
- **Interesting Facts**: "Wu Zhu" means "five grains" (weight standard)
- **Educational Summary**: Shows Han economic unification
- **References**:
  - "Chinese Numismatics" by Peng Xinwei
  - National Museum catalog

### Ancient China Museum Manifesto
Ancient China's continuous civilization flourished through:
1. Bureaucratic standardization (writing, measurements, currency)
2. Philosophical synthesis (Confucianism, Daoism)
3. Technological leadership (paper, compass, seismograph)
4. Agricultural innovation (iron plows, crop rotation)
5. Infrastructure mastery (canals, roads, Great Wall)

This collection preserves the material legacy of these achievements that shaped world history.
