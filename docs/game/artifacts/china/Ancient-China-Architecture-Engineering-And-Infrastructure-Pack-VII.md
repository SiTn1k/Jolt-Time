# Ancient China Architecture, Engineering & Infrastructure Pack VII

## Artifact Catalog

### 1. Qin Great Wall Brick
- **Original Chinese Name**: 秦长城砖 (Qín Chángchéng Zhuān)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Qin Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 214 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Great Wall Museum, Badaling
- **Discovery Site**: Inner Mongolia section
- **Category**: Fortification
- **Material**: Fired clay
- **Dimensions**: 36 × 18 × 9 cm
- **Historical Description**: Standardized brick with Qin seal script markings
- **Engineering Context**: Demonstrates mass production for Qin unification projects
- **Historical Importance**: Earliest standardized building material in China
- **Discovery Story**: Excavated from collapsed wall section in 1987
- **Interesting Facts**: Contains fingerprint of original worker
- **Educational Summary**: Shows Qin engineering standardization
- **References**:
  - Great Wall archaeological reports
  - "Qin Construction Methods" by Wang Jian

### 2. Han Dynasty Dougong Bracket
- **Original Chinese Name**: 斗拱 (Dǒugǒng)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Han Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 1st century CE
- **Current Museum**: Shaanxi History Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Chang'an palace ruins
- **Category**: Architectural Component
- **Material**: Lacquered wood
- **Dimensions**: 42 × 28 × 18 cm
- **Historical Description**: Interlocking bracket for roof support
- **Engineering Context**: Key innovation allowing large roof overhangs
- **Historical Importance**: Defining feature of Chinese architecture
- **Discovery Story**: Found in 1999 palace excavation
- **Interesting Facts**: Precisely carved without nails
- **Educational Summary**: Demonstrates Han structural engineering
- **References**:
  - "Han Architecture" by Liu Xujie
  - Shaanxi excavation reports
