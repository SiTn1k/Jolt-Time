# Ancient China Religion & Philosophy Pack II

## Artifact Catalog

### 1. Bianzhong of Marquis Yi
- **Original Chinese Name**: 曾侯乙编钟 (Zēnghóu Yǐ Biānzhōng)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Warring States (Eastern Zhou)
- **Approximate Date**: 433 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Hubei Provincial Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Tomb of Marquis Yi, Suizhou, Hubei
- **Category**: Ritual Bells (Bianzhong)
- **Material**: Bronze
- **Dimensions**: Full set spans 7.48m wide, 2.65m tall
- **Historical Description**: 65-bell chime set for ritual music performances
- **Religious Context**: Used in state rituals and ancestor worship ceremonies
- **Historical Importance**: Earliest complete bianzhong set demonstrating advanced music theory
- **Discovery Story**: Found in 1978 in waterlogged tomb chamber
- **Interesting Facts**: Can play 5.5 octaves, still musically playable today
- **Educational Summary**: Shows integration of music in Zhou dynasty state religion
- **References**:
  - Hubei Provincial Museum catalog
  - "Music in the Age of Confucius" (2000)

### 2. Oracle Bone Divination Set
- **Original Chinese Name**: 鸟共鼠骨 (Niǎo Gōng Shǐ Gǔ)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Shang Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 1250-1046 BCE
- **Current Museum**: National Palace Museum, Taipei
- **Discovery Site**: Yinxu, Anyang
- **Category**: Oracle Ritual Objects
- **Material**: Ox scapula/turtle plastron
- **Dimensions**: Varies (typical 15-20cm)
- **Historical Description**: Divination tools for royal ancestor communication
- **Religious Context**: Central to Shang state religion and royal decision-making
- **Historical Importance**: Earliest Chinese writing samples
- **Discovery Story**: First recognized in 1899 by Wang Yirong
- **Interesting Facts**: Over 150,000 fragments discovered
- **Educational Summary**: Demonstrates Shang dynasty theocratic governance
- **References**:
  - "Shang Divination" by David Keightley
  - National Palace Museum studies
