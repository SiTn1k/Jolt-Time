# Ancient China Daily Life & Society Pack IV

## Artifact Catalog

### 1. Mawangdui Silk Textile Fragment
- **Original Chinese Name**: 马王堆丝绸 (Mǎwángduī Sīchóu)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Han Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 168 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Hunan Provincial Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Mawangdui Tomb 1, Changsha
- **Category**: Textiles
- **Material**: Silk
- **Dimensions**: Various (largest 48 × 40 cm)
- **Historical Description**: Exquisitely woven silk with cloud and animal motifs
- **Daily Life Context**: Worn by elite women, demonstrates advanced Han textile technology
- **Historical Importance**: Best preserved early Chinese silk fabrics
- **Discovery Story**: Found in 1972 in airtight tomb of Lady Dai
- **Interesting Facts**: Contains 5 distinct weave patterns
- **Educational Summary**: Shows Han dynasty fashion and textile craftsmanship
- **References**:
  - Hunan Provincial Museum catalog
  - "Han Dynasty Textiles" by Zhao Feng

### 2. Han Lacquer Cosmetic Box
- **Original Chinese Name**: 漆奁 (Qī Lián)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Han Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 1st century BCE
- **Current Museum**: National Museum of China
- **Discovery Site**: Jiangling, Hubei
- **Category**: Personal Grooming
- **Material**: Lacquered wood
- **Dimensions**: 18 cm diameter
- **Historical Description**: Circular box with cloud motifs containing rouge and powder
- **Daily Life Context**: Used by noblewomen for cosmetics storage
- **Historical Importance**: Demonstrates Han lacquerware technology
- **Discovery Story**: Found in 1975 with other toilet articles
- **Interesting Facts**: Contains original cosmetic residues
- **Educational Summary**: Reveals Han beauty standards and craftsmanship
- **References**:
  - "Han Lacquerware" by Wu Hung
  - National Museum studies
