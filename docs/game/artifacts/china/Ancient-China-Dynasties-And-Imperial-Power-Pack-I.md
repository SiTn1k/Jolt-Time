## Artifact Catalog

### 1. Simuwu Ding
- **Original Chinese Name**: 司母戊鼎 (Sīmǔwù Dǐng)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Shang Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 13th-11th century BCE
- **Current Museum**: National Museum of China, Beijing
- **Discovery Site**: Anyang, Henan Province
- **Category**: Bronze Ritual Vessel
- **Material**: Bronze
- **Dimensions**: 133 cm tall, 110 cm long, 78 cm wide
- **Historical Description**: Largest ancient bronze vessel ever found, used for ceremonial offerings
- **Political Context**: Demonstrated royal authority and technological sophistication of Shang bronze casting
- **Historical Importance**: Key artifact for understanding Shang ritual practices
- **Discovery Story**: Excavated in 1939 from a Shang royal tomb
- **Interesting Facts**: Weighs 832.84 kg, took over 1000 workers to cast
- **Educational Summary**: Exemplifies Shang bronze metallurgy and royal ancestor worship
- **References**: 
  - National Museum of China catalog
  - "Shang Ritual Bronzes" by Li Xueqin
