# Ancient China Science, Medicine & Inventions Pack V

## Artifact Catalog

### 1. Mawangdui Medical Manuscripts
- **Original Chinese Name**: 马王堆医书 (Mǎwángduī Yīshū)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Han Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 168 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Hunan Provincial Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Mawangdui Tomb 3, Changsha
- **Category**: Medical Texts
- **Material**: Silk
- **Dimensions**: Various scrolls (~24 cm height)
- **Historical Description**: Earliest surviving Chinese medical texts covering diagnosis, prescriptions and theories
- **Scientific Context**: Contains pulse theory, moxibustion techniques and herbal formulas still used today
- **Historical Importance**: Foundation of Traditional Chinese Medicine systemization
- **Discovery Story**: Found in 1973 in sealed lacquer box
- **Interesting Facts**: Includes earliest known anatomical charts
- **Educational Summary**: Demonstrates advanced Han medical knowledge
- **References**:
  - Hunan Provincial Museum publications
  - "Early Chinese Medical Texts" by Donald Harper

### 2. Han Dynasty South-Pointing Spoon
- **Original Chinese Name**: 司南勺 (Sīnán Sháo)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Han Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 206 BCE–220 CE
- **Current Museum**: National Museum of China
- **Discovery Site**: Henan Province
- **Category**: Navigational Instrument
- **Material**: Bronze
- **Dimensions**: ~15 cm long
- **Historical Description**: Lodestone spoon that aligns with Earth's magnetic field
- **Scientific Context**: Precursor to compass, used for geomancy and orientation
- **Historical Importance**: Earliest magnetic direction-finding device
- **Discovery Story**: Found with other divination objects
- **Interesting Facts**: Mentioned in Han philosophical texts
- **Educational Summary**: Shows early understanding of magnetism
- **References**:
  - "Ancient Chinese Inventions" by Deng Yinke
  - National Museum catalog
