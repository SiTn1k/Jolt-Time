# Ancient China Writing & Administration Pack III

## Artifact Catalog

### 1. Tsinghua Bamboo Slips
- **Original Chinese Name**: 清华简 (Qīnghuá Jiǎn)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Warring States (Eastern Zhou)
- **Approximate Date**: 305–300 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Tsinghua University Art Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Unknown (acquired 2008)
- **Category**: Administrative/Historical Texts
- **Material**: Bamboo
- **Dimensions**: ~46 cm long, 1 cm wide (per slip)
- **Historical Description**: Collection of 2,388 bamboo slips containing 69 works
- **Administrative Context**: Includes earliest decimal multiplication table, historical records, and administrative manuals
- **Historical Importance**: Provides primary sources on pre-Qin government operations
- **Discovery Story**: Illegally excavated and donated to Tsinghua University
- **Interesting Facts**: Contains lost "Book of Documents" chapters
- **Educational Summary**: Reveals Warring States period bureaucratic practices
- **References**:
  - Tsinghua University publications
  - "Early Chinese Texts" by Edward Shaughnessy

### 2. Qin Dynasty Tiger Tally
- **Original Chinese Name**: 虎符 (Hǔfú)
- **Civilization**: Ancient China
- **Historical Period**: Qin Dynasty
- **Approximate Date**: 221–206 BCE
- **Current Museum**: Shaanxi History Museum
- **Discovery Site**: Xi'an region
- **Category**: Military/Administrative Tallies
- **Material**: Bronze
- **Dimensions**: ~9 cm long
- **Historical Description**: Split tiger-shaped military authorization token
- **Administrative Context**: Used to verify troop movement orders under Qin unification
- **Historical Importance**: Demonstrates Qin's centralized military control system
- **Discovery Story**: Found with other Qin administrative artifacts
- **Interesting Facts**: Matching half held by commanding officer
- **Educational Summary**: Shows early standardization of military bureaucracy
- **References**:
  - Shaanxi archaeological reports
  - "Qin Administrative Documents" by Li Junming
