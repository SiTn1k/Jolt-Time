### Artifacts Preview

1. Name: Jadeite Mask of a Royal Ruler
   Original Maya Name: ?
   Civilization: Ancient Maya
   Historical Period: Late Classic
   Approximate Date: 700-800 CE
   Current Museum: Museo Nacional de Antropologia, Mexico City
   Discovery Site: Palenque
   Category: Royal Burial Artifact
   Material: Jadeite
   Dimensions: 24 cm x 19 cm
   Historical Description: This finely carved jadeite mask is believed to have belonged to a Maya king or high noble. Its intricate inlays represent royal symbolism.
   Historical Context: Jade masks were symbols of divine rulership and used in elite funerary rituals.
   Historical Importance: The artifact exemplifies the elite craftsmanship and spiritual beliefs of Maya royalty.
   Discovery Story: Excavated from the tomb of Pakal the Great in Palenque's Temple of the Inscriptions.
   Interesting Facts: The mask's green color symbolized fertility and renewal.
   Educational Summary: Reflects Maya royal burial customs and jade's ritual significance.
   References:
   - Museo Nacional de Antropologia catalog
   - Schele, Linda & Freidel, David (1990). "A Forest of Kings".
   - Database of Maya royal artifacts, University of Texas

1. Name: Dresden Codex Fragment
   Original Maya Name: ?
   Civilization: Ancient Maya
   Historical Period: Late Classic
   Approximate Date: 1200 CE
   Current Museum: Saxon State Library, Dresden
   Discovery Site: Uncertain
   Category: Codex Fragment
   Material: Bark Paper
   Dimensions: 15 cm x 30 cm
   Historical Description: A surviving fragment of one of the few remaining Maya codices, containing astronomical tables and calendar information.
   Historical Context: The codices are crucial for understanding Maya mathematics and astrology.
   Historical Importance: Primary source for Maya calendrical and astronomical knowledge.

   Interesting Facts: One of only four known Maya codices.
   Educational Summary: Highlights Maya intellectual achievements in writing and astronomy.
