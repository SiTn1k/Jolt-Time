# Jolt Time — Lore

## The Story of Jolt Time

### The Beginning of Time

In the age before ages, when the universe was young, there existed the **Chronos Engine** — an ancient machine of impossible size that governed the flow of time itself. The Chronos Engine was built by the **Architects**, beings of pure intellect who shaped reality according to cosmic laws.

For eons, the Engine hummed perfectly, weaving the tapestry of time — creating stars, planets, civilizations. But even the Architects could not foresee the **Temporal Fracture**, a catastrophic event that shattered the Engine into countless fragments scattered across all of history.

### The Time Fragments

The Temporal Fracture scattered pieces of the Chronos Engine throughout human history. These fragments — called **Chrono Shards** — hold immense power: they can open portals to any era, grant knowledge of past civilizations, and reveal secrets buried in time.

But the shards are dormant, scattered, and their power must be awakened through understanding. Only those who truly learn the lessons of history can unlock their full potential.

### The Time Wanderers

You are a **Time Wanderer** — one of the rare individuals born with the gift of sensing Chrono Shards. Guided by the whispers of the dormant Architects, you have discovered your ability to perceive the fragments and use them to travel through time.

Your mission: journey through the great civilizations of humanity, recover the Chrono Shards, learn the wisdom of the ages, and one day — perhaps — restore the Chronos Engine and heal the Temporal Fracture.

## The Civilizations

### Era 1: Ancient Mesopotamia (3500 BCE)
The cradle of civilization. Here, between the Tigris and Euphrates, humanity took its first steps toward greatness. Cuneiform tablets, ziggurat temples, and the Code of Hammurabi await discovery.

**Key Artifacts:** Clay tablets, cylinder seals, temple offerings
**Historical Themes:** Writing, law, agriculture, early urban society
**Challenges:** Decoding ancient scripts, building irrigation systems, establishing trade routes

### Era 2: Ancient Egypt (2500 BCE)
The age of pyramids and pharaohs. Along the Nile, a civilization of unprecedented ambition rose and left monuments that would stand for millennia.

**Key Artifacts:** Pyramid stones, hieroglyphic scrolls, burial treasures
**Historical Themes:** Architecture, religion, bureaucracy, mathematics
**Challenges:** Construction puzzles, tomb mysteries, festival preparations

### Era 3: Ancient Greece (500 BCE)
The birthplace of democracy, philosophy, and the Olympic Games. City-states compete for glory while thinkers shape the foundations of Western thought.

**Key Artifacts:** Bronze armor, amphora vases, philosophical scrolls
**Historical Themes:** Democracy, philosophy, arts, military tactics
**Challenges:** Political debates, athletic competitions, trade negotiations

### Era 4: Roman Empire (100 CE)
The greatest empire the ancient world had ever seen. Roads, aqueducts, laws, and legions — Rome's influence echoes through millennia.

**Key Artifacts:** Legion standards, architectural blueprints, legal scrolls
**Historical Themes:** Engineering, governance, military, law
**Challenges:** Engineering projects, political intrigue, gladiatorial games

### Era 5: Medieval Europe (1200 CE)
An age of knights, castles, and cathedrals. Feudalism shapes society while cathedrals reach toward heaven and trade routes connect distant lands.

**Key Artifacts:** Knightly equipment, cathedral plans, guild tokens
**Historical Themes:** Feudalism, crusades, architecture, trade
**Challenges:** Castle sieges, plague management, guild politics

### Era 6: Renaissance Italy (1500 CE)
The rebirth of art, science, and human potential. Great masters create masterpieces while explorers chart new worlds.

**Key Artifacts:** Artistic sketches, scientific instruments, maps
**Historical Themes:** Art, science, exploration, humanism
**Challenges:** Art commissions, scientific discoveries, navigation puzzles

## The Antagonists

### The Fractured
Not all who seek the Chrono Shards do so with pure intentions. The **Fractured** are corrupted Time Wanderers who seek to exploit the shards' power for domination rather than understanding.

They believe that controlling time means controlling destiny, and they will stop at nothing to gather shards for themselves. They are the primary antagonists of Jolt Time.

### The Eclipsed
Ancient beings who exist outside of time, the **Eclipsed** were once protectors of the Chronos Engine but were corrupted by the Temporal Fracture itself. They seek to prevent anyone from restoring the Engine — for if time flows freely again, they would cease to exist.

### The Void Seekers
A shadowy organization in the modern era that believes the Temporal Fracture was not an accident but a deliberate act. They seek to prevent the Engine's restoration, believing that fragmented time serves their purposes.

## The Artifacts

Each civilization holds powerful Chrono Shards, but they are guarded by trials, puzzles, and challenges. To claim a shard, players must:

1. **Discover** — Find the shard's location through exploration
2. **Learn** — Understand the historical context
3. **Prove** — Complete challenges that demonstrate mastery
4. **Claim** — Add the shard to their collection

Collected shards power the Time Wanderer's abilities and unlock new areas, abilities, and story content.

## The Endgame

When all Chrono Shards are collected and the Time Wanderer has learned the wisdom of every age, they face the ultimate choice:

Restore the Chronos Engine and heal the Temporal Fracture — but in doing so, erase the fragments' power and return time to its natural flow — or preserve the shards' power and become a guardian of time itself.

This choice shapes the ending and determines the Time Wanderer's legacy.
