# Jolt Time — Target Audience

## Primary Audience Segments

### 1. History Enthusiasts (25% of players)
**Age Range:** 18-45
**Demographics:** Students, educators, history buffs, museumgoers
**Motivation:** Deep interest in historical content, learning through play
**Behavior:** High engagement with lore, long session times, active exploration

**What they want:**
- Historically accurate content
- Detailed information about civilizations
- Hidden secrets and Easter eggs
- Academic depth in accessible format

**Monetization tolerance:** Moderate — will watch ads for bonus content
**Retention drivers:** New civilizations, historical accuracy, collection completion

### 2. Casual Gamers (35% of players)
**Age Range:** 16-40
**Demographics:** Mobile gamers, Telegram users, commuters
**Motivation:** Quick entertainment, brain training, stress relief
**Behavior:** Short sessions (5-15 min), regular return patterns, social sharing

**What they want:**
- Easy-to-understand gameplay
- Satisfying progression
- Beautiful visuals
- Quick wins and rewards

**Monetization tolerance:** Low to moderate — will watch occasional ad for rewards
**Retention drivers:** Daily rewards, streak bonuses, simple daily challenges

### 3. Educational Players (20% of players)
**Age Range:** 10-25
**Demographics:** Students, parents with children, educators
**Motivation:** Supplement learning, homework help, educational value
**Behavior:** Scheduled play sessions, parent-monitored, curriculum-aligned

**What they want:**
- Clear educational objectives
- Age-appropriate content
- Progress tracking
- Safe environment

**Monetization tolerance:** Low — prefer ad-free educational experience
**Retention drivers:** Learning achievements, certificate generation, parental involvement

### 4. Achievement Hunters (15% of players)
**Age Range:** 16-35
**Demographics:** Completionists, perfectionists, competitive players
**Motivation:** 100% completion, leaderboard position, collectibles
**Behavior:** Long sessions, grinding for rewards, extensive exploration

**What they want:**
- Clear achievement tracking
- Rare collectibles
- Leaderboard rankings
- Hidden achievements to discover

**Monetization tolerance:** High — will pay for convenience or exclusive items
**Retention drivers:** New achievements, limited-time events, exclusive rewards

### 5. Social Players (5% of players)
**Age Range:** 16-30
**Demographics:** Social media users, friend groups, community-oriented
**Motivation:** Playing with friends, sharing achievements, community belonging
**Behavior:** Co-op play when available, frequent sharing, community events

**What they want:**
- Friend leaderboards
- Gift mechanics
- Community challenges
- Shareable moments

**Monetization tolerance:** Moderate — will spend on gifts and social features
**Retention drivers:** Social features, friend activity, community events

## Secondary Audiences

### Parents and Educators
- Looking for safe, educational entertainment for children
- Value parental controls and progress visibility
- Appreciate learning outcome tracking
- Will recommend to other parents

### Retro/Nostalgia Players
- Appreciate historical aesthetics
- Enjoy "what if" scenarios
- Value attention to period details
- Will engage with deep lore

## Audience Insights

- 25% Western Europe
- 20% North America
- 10% Asia
- 5% Rest of World

### Platform Distribution
- 90% Mobile (Telegram on iOS/Android)
- 10% Tablet/Desktop (web version)

### Device Capabilities
- Design for mid-range devices
- Support both light and dark themes
- Account for varying screen sizes
- Optimize for poor network conditions

## Player Journey Map

### New Player (Day 1-7)
1. **Discovery:** Friend recommendation, Telegram search, app store
2. **Onboarding:** Quick tutorial, first time travel, initial wonder
3. **Hook:** First shard collected, story intrigue, visual appeal
4. **Retention:** Daily rewards, easy first challenges, community feel

### Engaged Player (Week 2-4)
1. **Exploration:** Multiple eras, deep lore, hidden content
2. **Mastery:** Optimizing strategies, achievement hunting
3. **Social:** Friend comparison, gifting, competition
4. **Retention:** Events, new content, progression milestones

### Core Player (Month 2+)
1. **Dedication:** 100% completion goals, community contribution
2. **Investment:** Time and potentially money into game
3. **Advocacy:** Recommending to friends, social sharing
4. **Retention:** Exclusive content, VIP treatment, influence on development

## Accessibility Considerations

### Visual Accessibility
- High contrast mode option
- Color blind friendly palette
- Scalable text sizes
- Screen reader compatibility

### Motor Accessibility
- One-handed play mode
- Adjustable interaction timing
- Voice control option
- Minimal precise tapping required

### Cognitive Accessibility
- Clear progression indicators
- Hint system for stuck players
- Adjustable difficulty
- Pause-anytime design

### Cultural Accessibility
- Neutral cultural references where possible
- Localization-ready design
- No culturally offensive content
- Inclusive representation

## Audience Growth Strategy

### Expansion Markets
- Develop localized content for key regions
- Partner with educational institutions
- Create teacher/student special features
- Build educational curriculum connections

### Retention Focus
- Never let progress feel meaningless
- Continuous small wins
- Regular meaningful updates
- Genuine community engagement
