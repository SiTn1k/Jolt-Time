# Jolt Time — Retention Systems Architecture

## Overview

**Purpose:** Define the complete architectural framework for the Retention Systems, establishing retention as a core business pillar that maximizes long-term engagement, reduces churn, increases lifetime value, and supports years of scalable growth.

**Scope:** This document establishes Retention as one of the most important strategic systems in the project, covering retention categories, philosophy, architecture layers, retention mechanisms, integration standards, analytics, segmentation, governance, and future expansion paths.

**Principle:** Retention should become one of the most important strategic systems in the project. The architecture must maximize long-term engagement, reduce churn, and increase lifetime value (LTV). Retention systems should be integrated across gameplay, social systems, progression, monetization, and community features.

---

## 1. Retention Categories

The Jolt Time retention ecosystem comprises seven distinct retention categories, each serving specific player lifecycle stages and engagement objectives.

### 1.1 Early Retention

**Definition:** Player retention focused on the critical first experience period (D0–D7).

**Core Objectives:**
- First-day engagement maximization
- Onboarding completion
- Activation milestone achievement
- Early progression loop establishment

**Success Metrics:**
- D1 retention rate
- Onboarding completion rate
- First session length
- Activation milestone rate

**Timeframe:** Days 0–7 of player lifecycle.

### 1.2 Progression Retention

**Definition:** Retention driven by meaningful progression systems and long-term advancement goals.

**Core Objectives:**
- Clear progression paths
- Milestone achievement
- Long-term goal setting
- Progression motivation maintenance

**Success Metrics:**
- Level advancement rate
- Milestone completion rate
- Progression session frequency
- Long-term player advancement

**Timeframe:** Ongoing throughout player lifecycle.

### 1.3 Collection Retention

**Definition:** Retention powered by artifact and museum collection systems.

**Core Objectives:**
- Artifact collection incentives
- Museum growth motivation
- Collection completion goals
- Collector identity development

**Success Metrics:**
- Artifact acquisition rate
- Collection completion percentage
- Museum expansion frequency
- Collection session duration

**Timeframe:** Ongoing throughout player lifecycle.

### 1.4 Social Retention

**Definition:** Retention strengthened through social connections and community participation.

**Core Objectives:**

- Community building

- Social recognition

**Success Metrics:**

- Social feature engagement

### 1.5 Seasonal Retention

**Definition:** Retention maintained through recurring seasonal content and events.

## 4. Early Retention Architecture

The Early Retention Architecture focuses on the critical first-week player experience.

### 4.1 First-Day Engagement

**Onboarding Flow:**
1. Welcome experience (D0, minutes 0–5)
2. Core loop introduction (D0, minutes 5–15)
3. First collection experience (D0, minutes 15–25)
4. Social introduction (D0, minutes 25–35)
5. Goal establishment (D0, minutes 35–45)
6. Independence transition (D0, minutes 45–60)

**First-Day Goals:**
- Complete tutorial sequence
- Make first artifact collection
- Complete first mission
- Join first guild (optional)
- Establish first daily habit

**Success Indicators:**
- Onboarding completion rate: 80%+
- First session length: 10+ minutes
- First-day return rate: 60%+

### 4.2 Onboarding Retention

**Onboarding Principles:**
- Frictionless first experience
- Immediate reward delivery
- Clear next-action guidance
- Progressive complexity introduction

**Onboarding Structure:**
- Step 1: Account creation (auto via Telegram)
- Step 2: Era selection (tutorial context)
- Step 3: First artifact collection
- Step 4: First mission completion
- Step 5: Museum introduction
- Step 6: Daily reward establishment
- Step 7: Social feature preview

**Completion Metrics:**
- Onboarding completion rate
- Time to first mission
- First reward delivery time
- Tutorial satisfaction score

### 4.3 Activation Milestones

**Activation Milestones:**
1. **First Collection** — Collect first artifact
2. **First Mission** — Complete first mission
3. **First Museum** — Visit museum
4. **First Daily** — Claim first daily reward
5. **First Friend** — Add first friend
6. **First Guild** — Join first guild
7. **First Level** — Reach level 2
8. **First Era** — Unlock second era

**Milestone Rewards:**
- Immediate in-game rewards
- Achievement badges
- Progress celebration
- Social sharing options

**Milestone Timing:**
- Milestones spread across D0–D7
- Never require leaving app
- Always achievable in session
- Clear visibility of next milestone

### 4.4 Early Progression Loops

**Core Loop Introduction:**
- Mission → Collect → Progress → Reward
- Daily → Reward → Mission → Collect
- Social → Share → Reward → Engage

**Loop Reinforcement:**
- Daily reminder timing
- Progress visibility
- Reward anticipation
- Loop completion celebration

**Loop Establishment:**
- First 3 days: Guided loop completion
- Days 4–7: Independent loop operation
- Day 7+: Self-sustaining loop expectation
